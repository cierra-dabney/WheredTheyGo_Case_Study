# “Where’dTheyGo?” Case Study 

Written by: Cierra S. Dabney


## Overview

---

“Where’dTheyGo?” is an Instagram insight tracker that provides users with actionable recommendations regarding their engagement trends, follower retention. and follower growth. To assist “Where’dTheyGo?” bridges the gap between creators and their audiences encouraging intentional social media interaction for sustainable growth through risk signals, actionable insights, and personalized recommendations.

## What’s the Problem?

---

Instagram users can see follower count and engagement metrics but often lack context for why those numbers change and what actions they should take to improve growth.

## Success Metrics

---

- Reduce time spent interpreting account performance
- Increase user understanding of engagement trends
- Increase confidence in content strategy decisions
- Encourage consistent posting behavior through recommendations

## User Research

---

I conducted informal interviews with students, aspiring content creators, small businesses, and small creators who actively use Instagram. All users shared the same sentiment: they appreciate Instagram’s analytics tools, but the struggled to connect engagement metrics with follower growth to improve their media performance.

## User Persona

---

**Name:** Francesca Knight

**Age:** 21

**Followers:** 1,120

**Posts per week:** 2

**Goal:** Grow personal brand while creating college content on Instagram and reach 5,000 followers in 1-year.

**Pain Point:** Doesn’t understand why she hasn’t seen follower growth depsite posting biweekly

## Solution

---

Users found the information gathered by Instagram helpful but not useful for growth. Where’dTheyGo fills the gap.

- Dashboard
    - Users can view their current follower amount, follower change, and engagement rate
    - Graph of daily follower growth and average engagement
    - Users can manually log their recent post to update a table view of recent posts showing: date, type, likes, comments, and change in followers
- Insights
    - Features a graph of net followers gained per post per day
    - Account health view to act as a quick summary
    - Risk signals from users posting history
    - Performance breakdown with averages of engagement from: photos, stories, and reels
- Recommendations
    - Growth deck with personalized recommendations and 30-day follower projection
- Accessibility
    - Added  inclusive navigation patterns, allowing direct access to dashboard content and bypassing navigation by pressing Tab

## Tradeoffs

---

| **Decision** | **Alternative** | **Reason** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Manual Data Input | Instagram API Integration | API restrictions would’ve increased development complexity and distracted from validating the core user problem. |
| Instagram Focus Only | Multi-platform Support | I narrowed the scope to Instagram (single platform) to validate the users problems before expansion. |
| Lovable for MVP UI Prioritization | Excel | Great for analysis and calculating abilities; however, Excel wouldn’t have offered the user experience I intended for. |
| Lovable for MVP UI Prioritization | Python | Python would have shifted focus to be technical instead of product focused and required significantly more time. |

## Reflection

---

Throughout this project, I had to take a step back from being technically focused and really place myself in the users shoes. Even when I explored Instagram’s analytics, I originally didn’t see anything that needed to be changed. “It looks fine,” was my first thought.

However, when I began myself how the experience could be improved for users, I came to the understanding that having data is only valuable if it helps people make better decisions. Without actionable insights, data is simply information being collected and at times rendered useless.

Although I don’t see myself entering the content creator space on Instagram, my informal user research demonstrated that "Where’dTheyGo?" addresses a real need. Ultimately, this project reinforced the importance of solving underlying user problems rather focusing solely on features. Users care more about understanding their engagement trends to support growth intentionally.
